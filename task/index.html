Coca-Cola, or Coke, is a carbonated soft drink manufactured by the Coca-Cola Company. In 1888 Pemberton sold Coca-Cola's ownership rights to Asa Griggs Candler, a businessman, whose marketing tactics led Coca-Cola to its dominance of the global soft-drink market throughout the 20th and 21st century. The drink's name refers to two of its original ingredients: coca leaves and kola nuts (a source of caffeine). The drink has inspired imitators and created a whole classification of soft drink: colas.
